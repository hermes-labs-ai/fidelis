# Cogito Retrieval R&D Plan — April 15, 2026

## Baseline (measured today)
LongMemEval S split, 443 questions:
- R@1 56.2%, R@5 81.3%, R@10 92.3%
- recall_all R@10 77.0%, NDCG@10 69.2%
- Avg latency: 75ms/question
- Architecture: recall_b (zero-LLM sub-query decomposition + RRF + cosine rerank via nomic-embed-text)
- Results: bench/results-2026-04-15.json

## Why R@1 Matters
R@10 92.3% means the answer is almost always in the candidates. R@1 56.2% means it's ranked first only half the time. The integer-pointer filter (Stage 2) can fix bad ranking — but better R@1 means less work for the filter and fewer false negatives.

## Improvement Tests (running/queued)

### Test A: Multi-query expansion (running)
Generate 3 query variants via qwen3.5:2b, search all 3, merge with RRF.
Expected: +5-10pp R@1 (different phrasings surface different matches)
Results → bench/results-multiquery-2026-04-15.json

### Test B: Two-pass reranking (running)
Rerank top-20 with bge-reranker-v2-m3 cross-encoder.
Expected: +5-15pp R@1 (cross-encoder is better ranker than cosine)
Results → bench/results-rerank-2026-04-15.json

### Test C: Scaffold-as-index (queued — after A+B finish)
Compress sessions into scaffold summaries before embedding. Denser, more queryable representations.
Expected: +3-8pp R@1 (removes noise, concentrates signal)
Results → bench/results-scaffold-index-2026-04-15.json

### Test D: Turn-level chunking (queued — after C)
Chunk sessions into turn pairs instead of whole sessions. More precise retrieval units.
Expected: +5-10pp R@1 (answer in one turn ranks higher than buried in 200-turn session)
Results → bench/results-turnlevel-2026-04-15.json

### Test E: Combine best of above (only if C+D both improve)
Stack the winning approaches. Expected ceiling: R@1 75-85%.

## No Published Baselines
MemPalace 96.6% claim is unverified and likely on a different benchmark/split. Backboard.io claims "#1 on LongMemEval" but publishes no numbers. mem0, MemGPT, Letta have no published LongMemEval results. Our numbers are honest but incomparable until someone runs the same benchmark.

## Connection to LPCI
Scaffold-as-index (Test C) is the LPCI thesis applied to retrieval: compress language state into a scaffold that preserves Markov sufficiency, use it for downstream tasks. If R@1 improves, it's empirical evidence that scaffold compression preserves retrieval-relevant state.
